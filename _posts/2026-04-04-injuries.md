title: "Crushing Loss, Untimely Injuries Derail Lakers’ Momentum"
date: 2026-04-04
---

Coming back from the road trip, the Lakers finished with convincing wins over some of the Eastern Conference’s weakest teams in the [Nets](https://www.espn.com/nba/game/_/gameId/401810930/nets-lakers) and [Wizards](https://www.espn.com/nba/game/_/gameId/401810953/wizards-lakers). Coming back from the road trip, the team certainly appeared fatigued, especially at the starts of these games. But importantly, they found a way to finish the games and end up with a win. With these two games out of the way, the team headed into a stretch of games that would be a good litmus test for how it performs against playoff caliber teams.

On Tuesday, the Cavaliers came to town. Still in the hunt for a top three seed in the East, the fourth-seeded Cavs had good reason to play for a win. Despite Jarrett Allen’s quick start in the first half, the Lakers’ defense completely shut down both Donovan Mitchell and James Harden. Notably the Lakers’ best defender, Marcus Smart, has been out for the past few games with an ankle injury, which many fans are beginning to worry might be more severe than initially expected. Despite this, there wasn’t a significant drop in the team’s defense to end March.

Luka Dončić was continuing his [MVP-caliber season](https://www.espn.com/video/clip/_/id/48353743), finishing with 42 points against the Cavs after his [suspension against the Wizards](https://www.espn.com/nba/recap/_/gameId/401810953). He finished March with a [historic 600 total points](https://www.latimes.com/sports/lakers/story/2026-03-31/los-angeles-lakers-luka-doncic-milestone-cleveland-guardians), leading the team to what was looking like a fantastic regular season finish in preparation for a deep playoff run.

But with just one game, the entire season was lost. On Thursday, the Lakers were in Oklahoma City to take on the Thunder in a pivotal point of the season. This was a true test of whether the team was able to stay competitive against the best team in the league.

From the moment the game started, the Lakers looked completely out of their element. The defense that had been so solid for the entirety of the month of March was effortlessly dismantled by the Thunder. Much of this explosion of offense from the Thunder was due to their improbably high three point percentage, especially in the first half. Lu Dort, who is shooting just 33% from three this season, was 4-for-4 on threes in just the first quarter. Although this was simply an incredibly lucky shooting night for players like Dort, the Lakers defense should get most of the blame for allowing such wide open looks. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and Jalen Williams were able to get past Lakers defenders into the paint with little resistance throughout the entire game.

Honestly, I’m not sure if the team was just fatigued or the Thunder’s offense is just this good (likely a combination of the two), but the Lakers simply looked like a different team on Thursday compared to how they’ve been playing for over almost a month.

In the end, the Thunder handed the Lakers their worst loss of the season with a [139-96 win](https://www.espn.com/nba/boxscore/_/gameId/401810971). The final score alone would not have been the end of the season – the Lakers would have had another chance to avenge the loss in another game against the Thunder next week – but unfortunately, the 43-point loss was not the worst thing to happen in this game.

In the first quarter, Austin Reaves seemed to suffer an injury to his lower back and appeared to be in some discomfort throughout the remainder of the game. He was repeatedly seen holding his lower back as he ran up and down the court. Then, in the third quarter, Dončić left the game with a non-contact hamstring injury and did not return. After the game on Friday, the team revealed that [Dončić suffered a Grade 2 hamstring strain](https://www.espn.com/nba/story/_/id/48389387/lakers-doncic-hamstring-miss-least-rest-regular-season). On Saturday, news broke of [Reaves’ Grade 2 oblique injury](https://www.espn.com/contributor/shams-charania/e80d0629084d3). Both of the Lakers’ top two scorers are now out for the remainder of the regular season with their status for the playoffs still unknown. This leaves a 41-year-old LeBron James to carry the team for the rest of the season in the extremely competitive Western Conference.

Dončić and Reaves are undoubtedly two of the three most crucial players on the team. With both sidelined for the rest of the regular season, the winning streak and momentum built up through the month of March to be in position to grab the third seed in the West has gone to waste. Of course, LeBron James is still one of the best players in the NBA. However, carrying a team without any other primary playmakers at his age is a lot to ask, even of the greatest player of this generation. It wouldn’t surprise me if LeBron kept the team afloat into the playoffs until Dončić and/or Reaves returns, but without the two players that take on most of the offensive responsibilities for this team, it will be a daunting uphill battle.

With how good the team has been playing recently, it’s even more unfortunate that this one game has completely shifted my outlook on the season. I can see this team finishing the final five games of the season with maybe one or two losses, but I’m skeptical about the chances of advancing past the first round without the team’s two best scorers. The Lakers looked to be in contention for a championship this season after playing so well in March. But these injuries have [devastated the team’s hopes for a deep playoff run](https://www.espn.com/espn/betting/story/_/id/45539787/2025-nba-finals-odds-mvp-2025-26-championship-betting) and might have cost them a chance to compete for a title.

It looks like the Lakers season will once again be over earlier than expected. I think that the next few games will show what the team might be capable of without Dončić and Reaves, but many fans seem to be looking towards the offseason to revamp the team in preparation for next season.

As for the rest of this season, the team’s fate now lies in the hands of LeBron James in his 23rd year in the NBA.
