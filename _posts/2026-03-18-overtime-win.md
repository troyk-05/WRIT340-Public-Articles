In arguably the most important game of the season thus far, the Lakers pulled off an incredibly close overtime win against the Denver Nuggets on Saturday. The clutch play of [Austin Reaves and Luka Dončić](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jPuoeFailq8) in the fourth quarter and overtime made this one of the most entertaining games this season. This was the first overtime game for the Lakers this season, and reaffirmed the team’s identity as one of the most clutch teams in the NBA. My hope is that this identity will carry into the playoffs, where the team will undoubtedly find themselves in close games like this.

With this win, the Lakers also secured the head-to-head tiebreaker over the Nuggets, which could have significant implications for the playoff picture. The Lakers, Nuggets, Rockets, and Timberwolves are separated by just one game in the Western Conference standings. It would be in the best interest of the Lakers to secure the third seed in the west (just behind the Thunder and Spurs), as it would guarantee home-court advantage in the playoffs for at least the first round.

This week, the Lakers played two consecutive games in Houston who was just a half game behind the Lakers in the standings. It was imperative that this mini-series was treated like playoff games. I expected a 1-1 split with the Rockets in these games, but it was clear after watching these games that JJ Redick simply outcoached Ime Udoka, propelling the Lakers to steal both crucial games on the road. The winning streak has now been extended to seven games, but more importantly, the Lakers [secured the tiebreaker over the Rockets](https://www.silverscreenandroll.com/lakers-analysis/109731/lakers-tiebreakers-rockets-nuggets-wolves-warriors-suns-western-conference-standings-playoff-race).

These wins against the Nuggets and Rockets have given me more confidence in this team as the end of the season approaches. Compared to last season, Luka Dončić’s impact is much more prominent. Around this time last year, it still felt as if he was still dealing with the shock of the trade. However, a full season later, he has fully committed to this team and is beginning to return to his 2024 form.

At several points this season, I could clearly recognize that this team is not perfectly built to fit Dončić’s game. There are weaknesses with defense and shooting that don’t fully maximize the potential of a team led by Luka Dončić, but over the past few games, coach Redick has illustrated his ability to develop and implement defensive schemes to hide some of the team’s shortcomings.

In the games against the Rockets, there were notable shifts to zone defense sets. Although these sets come with their own challenges, the Lakers seemed very prepared and well-coordinated with their movements to eliminate the weaknesses of zone defense. The on-ball defensive weaknesses of Dončić and Reaves can be hidden with a well-organized and practiced zone, which seems to be what the Lakers have been implementing over the past few games. Strategic uses of double teams on Kevin Durant allowed the Lakers to take advantage of the Rockets’ lack of playmaking outside of Durant. The YouTube channel BBallBreakdown recently published a [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UDSVXSZjQRs) which I believe perfectly explains what the Lakers did to take these two games against Houston. This strategy might not be effective against teams with stronger offenses, but I have confidence in Redick’s abilities to craft schemes for each team on the schedule.

Looking forward, the road trip continues in Miami tomorrow, Orlando on Saturday, Detroit on Monday, and finally ends in Indiana next Wednesday. It is unfortunate that the Lakers play in Houston and Miami on back-to-back days. I wouldn’t be surprised if Redick chooses to rest the starters to prepare for the rest of the road trip.

With the team staying hot on this win streak and many of the remaining games being very winnable, the Lakers seem to be in great shape heading into the end of the season.
